How to prepare Smoke Oven Catfish

To prepare a smoke oven catfish, start by cleaning the fish thoroughly, removing scales, gutting, and washing with lime, salt, or vinegar to eliminate slime and odor. Make deep cuts on the fish for better seasoning penetration. Marinate with a mix of salt, spices, and seasonings like ginger, garlic, and pepper, then let it rest for a few hours. Preheat the smoking oven and arrange the fish on the grill or rack, ensuring proper air circulation. Use hardwood or charcoal for an even, slow smoke, maintaining a moderate temperature (around 80-120Â°C). Smoke for several hours (4-8 hours) until the fish is dry, firm, and golden brown. Allow it to cool before storage or serving.

How to make Kulikuli

To make kulikuli spices, blend together dried ginger, garlic, onions, and chili pepper into a fine powder. Add salt, a little sugar (optional), and other seasonings like cloves, nutmeg, or African pepper for extra flavor. Roast the mixture lightly to enhance its aroma, then store in an airtight container. This spice mix is used to season groundnut paste before frying kulikuli, giving it a rich, spicy taste.

Cooking tips for Ewedu Powder

To cook blended ewedu powder, boil 1 cup of water in a pot. Gradually sprinkle the ewedu powder into the boiling water while stirring continuously to prevent lumps. Use a whisk (ijabe) or hand blender to mix until smooth. Add salt, seasoning cubes, ground crayfish, and locust beans (iru) for extra flavor. Let it simmer for 3-5 minutes on low heat, then serve hot with amala, tuwo, or fufu.

Cooking tips for making Powder Pap

To cook powdered pap, mix 2-3 tablespoons of the powder with a little cold water in a bowl to form a smooth paste. Boil water separately and gradually pour it into the paste while stirring continuously until it thickens. If needed, place the mixture on low heat and stir for 1-2 minutes for better consistency. Add sugar, honey, or milk to taste, then serve hot with akara, moimoi, or bread.

How to cook Poundo Yam

To prepare Poundo Yam, boil water in a pot until it reaches a rolling boil. Gradually add Poundo Yam flour while stirring continuously with a wooden spatula to prevent lumps. Keep stirring until the mixture becomes smooth, stretchy, and firm. If it's too thick, add a little hot water and mix well; if too soft, sprinkle more flour and stir until desired consistency is achieved. Allow it to cook for a few minutes on low heat, then serve hot with soups like egusi, ogbono, or efo riro.
